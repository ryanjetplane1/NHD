What is a nuke?

A nuke is an explosive device deriving its force from a nuclear reaction, which split or merge heavy atomic nuclei, releasing massive energy in a catastrophic explosion. It causes blast waves, intense heat, deadly radiation, and long-lasting environmental contamination. Nuclear weapons are considered the deadliest and most catastrophic ever, leading to global efforts to disarm and prevent their spread.
