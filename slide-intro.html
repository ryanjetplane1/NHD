What is a nuke?

A nuclear weapon, commonly referred to as a nuke, is an immensely powerful explosive device that derives its destructive force from the release of energy during a nuclear reaction. It involves a process called nuclear fission or fusion, where the atomic nuclei of certain heavy elements are split or merged, resulting in an intense release of energy. This energy is unleashed in the form of a catastrophic explosion, producing an enormous blast wave, intense heat, and a deadly radiation pulse. Nukes are capable of causing widespread devastation, emitting a mushroom cloud that can level entire cities, leaving behind profound destruction and long-term environmental contamination. Due to their immense destructive power, nuclear weapons are considered the most deadly and catastrophic weapons ever created, leading to global efforts aimed at nuclear disarmament and non-proliferation.
